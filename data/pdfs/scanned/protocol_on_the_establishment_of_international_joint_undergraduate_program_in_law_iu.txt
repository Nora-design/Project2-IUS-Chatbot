ARTICLE 17 — Execution of the Programme

(1) At IU, academic procedures regarding the program will be carried out by the program
coordinator, and administrative procedures will be carried out by the Student Affairs
Department.

(2) Program-related requirements, academic and other procedures at IUS will be carried out
by the [US International Relations Office in cooperation with the TU Student Affairs
Department.

(3) Within the scope of the execution of this Protocol, the IU Student Affairs Department
Head and the IUS Internationa! Relations Office Director will be the designated
representatives of the parties. All activity documents. notes. warnings, problems.
administrative issues and correspondence regarding this protocol will be forwarded to
the relevant representatives.

(4) Changes in 1U and IUS representatives will be notified to the other party as soon as
possible. All documents requiring corporate signature are signed by representatives of
the Parties authorized to sign.

(5) Student information and transcripts will be notified to the host institution by the
University at the beginning and end of the semester, starting from the stage when
students enroll in the program.

ARTICLE 18 — Curriculum

(1) The IJBDP curriculum, its definitions, and the sharing of the activities that constitute
the program such as courses, laboratories, practices, internships and thesis, in return for
credit and between IUS and IU, and course contents are included in ANNEX 2.

(2) IJBDP consists of 240 ECTS in accordance with the foundations of the Turkish Higher
Education Qualifications Framework and the Bosnia-Herzegovina Qualifications

Framework.

(3) To ensure that the courses offered at both institutions are compatible in terms of content
and structure, the relevant departments of both institutions will review and approve all
required courses offered within the program. Institutions will maintain communication

on this issue and make these reviews or changes annually.

(4) For the purpose of approval of this Protocol by the competent authorities in Ttirkiye and
Bosnia-Herzegovina, the relevant departments, upon official request, will mutually send
to the other institution a list of competent academic staff related to their parts of the
curriculum, together with their CVs and academic promotion decisions (including
scientific fields and courses).

ARTICLE 19 — Grading System

(1) The student's academic success in each course and his overall academic success will be
determined according to the legislation of the institution where he studies.

(2) The student's transcript will be sent to both institutions at the end of each semester.
(3) [U's grading system is included in ANNEX 3.

(4) 1US's grading system is included in ANNEX 4.

ARTICLE 20 -

Continuation Provisions between [U-IUS Double Diploma Program

(1) Atthe end of two (2) academic years (four semesters) successfully completed, excluding
the English Preparation Programme, in academic, disciplinary and financial good
standing at each institution, as defined above, students will be able to transfer between
institutions, provided that the following conditions are met for the courses they have
taken to earn a Bachelor's Degree at both institutions: :

(a) In order for the student to transfer from IUS to IU, he/she must meet the transfer
requirements of both institutions. Therefore, students are required to comply
with all applicable requirements and time limits regarding admission,

orientation, registration, tuition and other fee payments.

(b) Students will comply with all applicable rules and processes of the institution in
which they are enrolled.

(c) Students who will transfer within the program must meet the Turkish language

proficiency requirements stated in ARTICLE 14.
ARTICLE 21 — Freezing of Registration

(1) IJBDP students may suspend their registration for a semester or an academic year based
on documented compelling circumstances and/or medical reasons, provided that they
complete the documents required by the institution they attend.

(2) The approved registration suspension period will not be included in the maximum
training period.

(3) The total period of suspension of registration cannot be longer than half of the legal
period foreseen by the program or cannot exceed the periods specified in the regulations
of the relevant institutions.

ARTICLE 22 — Disciplinary Rules

(1) Joint degree program students will be subject to the Disciplinary’ Regulations of the

University where they are studying.

(2) In terms of academic functioning, each institution has sole and exclusive authority over
all student conduct and discipline matters, including grade objections, accusations of
cheating, plagiarism and violation of classroom rules.

(3) The parties prepare an orientation program or material for the student before
participating in the joint degree program and inform the student about applicable
policies covering issues such as academic responsibilities and discipline.

ARTICLE 23 — Horizontal Transfer

(1) A Joint Undergraduate Program student who is studying in an equivalent program at [U
or 1US may apply to transfer from the joint undergraduate program to another
international joint undergraduate program in the same field within the same university
or another university, if he/she has at least 3.0 GPA (out of 4.0) in his/her current
program, in accordance with the "Regulation on the Principles of Transferring Between
Associate and Undergraduate Programs, Double Major, Minor and Inter-Institutional
Credit Transfer in Higher Education Institutions" and the conditions of the institution to
which transfer is requested.

(2) Transfers of TU students from IJBDP to another program in Tiirkiye are made in

accordance with the regulations specified in the first paragraph of this article.
(3) Transfers of TUS students from IJBDP to another program within Bosnia and
Herzegovina or to an international program are made in accordance with the TUS
transfer regulations and with the knowledge of IU.

ARTICLE 24 — Visa Procedures and Student Residence Permit

(1) Students participating in the program are responsible for all matters and payments
related to applying for and obtaining a student visa required for study and entry to the
country. The student is also responsible for all expenses such as living and other personal
needs, accommodation, travel, and all payments related to the return process to the
country in case the student is expelled due to academic failure or non-compliance.

(2) After being accepted to IJBDP, students will be considered as actively registered
students at the institution where they are physically present. and as registered but on
leave students at the institution where they are not physically present. Students at IUS
will apply far 2 residence permit in Bosnia and Herzeges ine on an annual basis until
they complete their second academic year (this period may also include the English
Preparatory Programme).

(3) Each institution is responsible for keeping records about the students attending the
IJBDP program within its structure and informing the other institution. IUS does not
make any promises or guarantees about obtaining the necessary residence permits for
students during their study in Bosnia and Herzegovina. Students who are citizens of
Bosnia and Herzegovina are required to obtain a student residence permit in Tiirkiye.
IU does not make any promises or guarantees about obtaining the necessary residence
permits for students during their study period in Tiirkiye.

(1) Any matter not expressly stated in this Protocol will be dealt with in accordance with

ARTICLE 26 — Duration and Validity

(1) This Protocol is valid for five (5) years starting from the academic year following its
approval by official authorities and can be renewed at the end of every five (5) years by
mutual written Protocol or agreement of the parties and with the approval of YOK.
ARTICLE 27 — Termination

(1) Parties may terminate this Protocol by notifying the other institution in writing at least
ninety (90) days before the end of any academic semester. This early termination notice
will be effective for the next academic semester and will not impose any further
responsibility or liability on the other institution. Provisions that by their nature will
continue to be valid after the termination or completion of this Protocol will survive the
termination and/or completion of this Protocol.

(2) Unless the parties notify of termination, the Higher Education Council approval process
will restart for the extension of the Protocol under the same conditions.

(3) In the event of completion and/or early termination of this Protocol, both institutions
undertake to create a “Program Completion” plan that will not affect students currently
enrolled in the IJBDP program. This plan may include various transfer options or
program completion methods in accordance with the framework of the Protocol.
Institutions agree that early termination of the Protocol should be done through joint
consultation and all students who wili be attectea by this situation should be notified

within fifteen (15) business days and appropriate options should be offered.
ARTICLE 28 — Resolution of Disputes

(1) Any disputes arising from the interpretation, amendments, implementation or violation
of this Protocol will be settled amicably by negotiation between the partner institutions.

ARTICLE 29 — Confidentiality

(1) Confidential Information: Institutions will securely store any information that is
marked, identified and/or understood to be confidential within reasonable limits by the
other institution during sharing, except for cases that are necessary for the execution of
this Protocol or that are required to be shared if requested by law or legal processes.

(2) Student Registry Files: Both institutions state that IUS is obliged to act in accordance
with the Sarajevo Canton Higher Education Law and the IUS status approved by the
Sarajevo Canton Ministry of Education, Science and Youth in keeping the educational
records of students registered at IUS and accept that IUS may have to make changes
from time to time. Sharing of student registry records between the two institutions will
be carried out in accordance with local privacy laws, and if necessary, the host

registry records. All student registry records will be used by institutions only for
registration, student admission and academic matters.

(1) Student's final achievements shown in column Letter Grade in B&H as above are graded
in linewith the following criteria:

a) 10(A)— exceptional achievement, without errors, or with minimal errors, 95 — 100
points;

b) 9(B)-—above average achievement, with a few errors, 85 — 94 points;

c) 8(C)—average achievement, with noticeable errors, 75 — 84 points;

d) 7(D)~-generally good achievement, with significant imperfections, 65 — 74 points:

e) 6(E)—meets minimal criteria for achievement, 55 — 64 points;

f) 5 (F, FX)—does not meet minimal criteria, less than 55 points.

(2) Letter marks which are not affecting student’s CGPA:

a) “IP”-In progress is assigned for recording unfulfilled student obligations related to
thesispreparation.

b) “S” — Satisfactory is assigned to student who passed the examinations that
are notnumerically graded, or whose written assignment has been accepted.

c) “U”— Unsatisfactory is assigned to student who failed to pass the examinations
that arenot numerically graded.

d) “W”— Withdrawal signifies that student has withdrawn from the relevant course.

Additional letter mark that affects student’s CGPA is “N/A” — Not attending and it is assigned

to student who is suspended from the course or who does not meet minimal requirement for
attendanceon lectures or tutorials.
